I Can (Not) Create a Kinetic Novel


What is a kinetic novel? A kinetic novel is a type of video game and a sub-category of visual novel. A visual novel is a game that displays contains writing similar to a novel and is interactive. Lines of text are displayed individually on command and are accompanied by music and background visuals. The defining feature of a visual novel is the ability to make choices that impact the narrative. These choices lead to the player navigating different story "routes". A kinetic novel lacks the addition of different routes and instead has only one "true route".



In an attempt to expand my skills with the language GML (which was written in C++ & C#) beyond the realm of top-down adventure games and old-school shoot 'em ups, I decided to try making my own kinetic novel. I was only interested in the coding aspect of the creation and decided not to write an original story, and instead to adapt the already existing short-story "A tell-Tale Heart" by Edgar Allan Poe into a kinetic novel.



I opted to do go with an early 00's look for my game with a box ratio for the screen and (un)intentional lack of features besides the text istelf. The background images were sourced online and altered via Photoshop by me to create a consistant atmosphere to complement the dark tones of the story. The 3 sound tracks found in the game were ripped from YouTube and put in the game.
